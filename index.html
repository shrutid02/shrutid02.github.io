About me

I’m a passionate software engineer with 4 years of professional experience in software development, and a Master’s degree in Computer Science from North Carolina State University. I embarked on my professional journey in January 2020, as a Software Engineer at Fivetran, where I led several backend-focused projects to enhance Fivetran’s File-based and Kafka-based connector modules.



After starting my Master’s coursework, I worked at Trestle as a Software Engineer Intern, where I developed a secure pay-as-you-go wallet solution, enabling customers to top up their wallets and use them to consume Trestle’s API products. Currently, I’m working at the National Science Foundation’s CIPM department at NCSU, where I lead the development of a pest management and trap design website.



I believe my greatest strengths are adapting to new technologies, adjusting to changing requirements, and delivering tasks efficiently. As a software engineer, I constantly aim enhance my skillset and utilize it to create solutions that genuinely impact users and drive business growth.
